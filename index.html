Finding out I was called to Serve in Japan

As I was working on my mission papers, I had no idea where I was going to be called.

Posted by Emily Keith on January 30, 2024

There were many places that went through my head as I was working on my mission papers: Argentina, Texas, Panama, California, but Japan had never run through my head. Ten days before I recieved my call, I woke up and thought to myself "Japan!" I didn't think of it ever again, but I wrote it down in my dream journal. I forgot I had the dream and never thought of it again.

Ten days later right before I opened my misison call my brother told me that if I was called to any of the places he had hot sauces from, he would give the hot sauce. We read through all of them without missing a beat we read through the hot sauces and didn't think anything of it.

As I read through my call in front of my immediate family I was very emotional and as I read the words "Japan Sendai Mission" I had tunnel vision. I didn't even realize all of the little moments leading up to Japan.

As I read it I immediately knew that it was where I was meant to be! I'm so grateful for this opportunity to serve, and although I know it will be hard I am so excited and have faith that it will all work out!

How to Prepare for Your Mission to Sendai

Posted by Emily Keith on April 16, 2024

There are many different aspects to prepare for in order to be ready for a mission. Specifically to Japan, here are some ideas:

  1. Aquire a passport and visa.
  2. Familiarize yourself with the culture.
  3. Begin studying the language.
  4. Check missionary email regularly.
  5. Complete missionary technology course.
  6. Research and begin purchasing clothing and other items.
  7. Completely read the Book of Mormon.
  8. Gather and clean personal items.
  9. Attend the temple regularly.
  10. Make a plan for when you get back.
